Digitalizing the Catalogus Baronum

The first page of the catalog in its 17th-century reprinting.

Introduction

The Catalogus Baronum or Catalogue of Barons, is a Latin register that records the military obligation owed by the lord and barons within the 12th-century Kingdom of Sicily. The register was created under the first king Roger II in the 12th century and expanded during the thirteenth. The registry contains over a thousand entries that include the names of lords, the sum of their obligations and where their fiefs were located. These fees were articulated as knights and soldiers and were managed by either a constable or a count. This project and website seeks to make accessing some of these entries easier.

The Project

The Catalogus Baronum was written entirely in Latin and currently lacks a database of any sort. One would need to translate and organize hundreds of entries to meaningfully use the register. Though difficult to access, the document provides information regarding the lordship, and royal administration of the mainland kingdom. The ability to access this information more easily allows scholars or interested internetgoers, who lack the extensive training required, to meaningfully interpret and contribute to a large conversation about the kingdom of Sicily. Using the modern critical edition by Evelyn Jamison, this project does just that and records the first 99 entries into a searchable database (these locations are organized by modern communal boundaries, with provided contemporary names). Accompanying the data base is a corresponding map that allows users to project and compare entries in realtime. Answering geographical questions regarding the distribution and fiefs and location of lords has been made significantly more intuitive and easier.
