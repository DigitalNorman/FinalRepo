I retrieved my information from the entries found within the critical edition by Evelyn Jamison. I used the ID numbers found in the book associated with each entry, which is referred to as the ‘Book_ID’ in the CSV file. Beyond transcribing the information found in the book.


When there were a series of feudums but without specificity to the communes, I divided the number evenly between them. For example, if there was a lord who had three territories and nine knights, I divided the number evenly between them. Each territorial entry received three knight fees owed. 


 Latin has declensions which change the ending of nouns. I have adjusted all the names of territories and individuals so that their name appears in the nominative in the list.


 I assigned a lord ID to each individual which is different from the ID in the book. There are several lords who own more than a single territory and appear multiple times. 
 

 If a lord appears several times, they only receive one entry and ID in the list but multiple ‘Book_ IDs'. Collections of individuals, or those not given clear names, will have their titles placed in the ¨surname¨ category. The original text is taken directly from the critical edition.
