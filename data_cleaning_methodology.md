I retrieved my information from the entries found within the critical edition by Evelyn Jamison (citations can be found in the sustainability page). I used the ID numbers found in the book associated with each entry, which is referred to as the ‘Book_ID’ in the CSV file, to identify passages.


When entires noted a series of feudums without specificity to which communes, I divided the number evenly between them. For example, if there was a lord who had three territories and nine knights fees, I divided the number evenly between them. Each territorial entry received three knight fees. 


 Latin has declensions which change the ending of nouns. I have adjusted all the names of territories and individuals so that their name appears in the nominative (the subject case) in the list.


 I assigned a 'lord_ID' to each individual which is different from the 'Book_ID'. There are several lords who own more than a single territory and appear multiple times in different entries. This prevents duplicates from appearing in the table.
 
 Collections of individuals, or those not given clear names, will have their titles placed in the ¨surname¨ category. The original text is taken directly from the critical edition.
